The story

I started as the only designer at a startup that didn't know what a designer was supposed to do. I figured it out fast. We shipped, we iterated, we grew — and I learned that the gap between a good idea and a shipped product is where design actually happens.

From there I joined a scale-up where I became a staff-level IC, ran the design system project, and started managing designers unofficially before the title caught up. Management wasn't a career pivot — it was a natural extension of how I'd always worked.

Now I lead a design team. I still get into Figma. I still run user research sessions. I still argue about typography. I just also hire, mentor, structure teams, and make sure the design function has a seat at the table it deserves.

What I'm looking for next: a company where design actually matters, a problem worth solving, and a team worth building. Senior IC, management, or director — I'm open to all three if the fit is right.

What I believe

Design principles.

01

Ship it

A perfect design that doesn't ship is a failure. I bias toward getting something real in front of real users and iterating from there.

02

Craft matters

Details aren't decoration — they're signals. Sloppy craft signals sloppy thinking. I care about the 1px things because they compound.

03

Measure it

Good taste and good data are not opposites. I define success metrics before I start designing and I use them to know when I'm done.

04

Systems thinking

One-off solutions create debt. I design for the pattern first, the instance second — whether I'm building a component or a design team.

05

Earn the seat

Design doesn't automatically have a seat at the table. You earn it by being right often enough and communicating well enough that people want you there.

06

People first

The best design output comes from psychologically safe teams. I manage to create conditions for good work, not to extract it.
